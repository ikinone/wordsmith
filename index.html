For Authors: The Value of Editing

Whatever you write about – from dead reckoning to eternal life – can be just as evocative. But to achieve this you need a good editor.

An editor acts as a mirror, reflecting your writing from an external perspective, revealing strengths and weaknesses that you can’t notice alone. Why should you worry about this?

"To write a book without an editor would be total madness. Caroline not only corrects my grammar and punctuation, but she checks the consistency of facts – names, places, people, dates. It would be embarrassing to reveal how many errors she finds, but I don’t think I am alone. We all need a second pair of eyes."

Matthew Woodward, author of The Railway to Heaven

An editor’s skill and experience enables them to adjust the text so that while your message retains your authorial voice, it is powerful and memorable as well.

Editing comes in two flavours:

  • Structural editing: this is the first of the two processes. The editor looks at the bigger picture, the overall structure of the book; they check its plot, character development and pacing. They ensure that the different parts of the story – in good non-fiction, too, there is always a story – fit together well, and that the beginning and ending are strong and memorable.
    In sum, a successful collaboration between you and your structural editor not only transforms your manuscript, but also helps you grow your writing skills.
  • Copyediting: this is the second process; a good copyeditor ensures that your text is clear, coherent, consistent and correct. Yes, this does mean they will correct errors in grammar, spelling, punctuation and style – but it also means they will check facts, see whether any author bias is unintentionally revealed, and ensure the text is in an appropriate linguistic register for its readers. And finally – an essential but invisible task – they will format the text ready for a design program or conversion into an e-book.

I’ve worked as an editor since the early nineties, and I’ll give you a friendly, helpful and cost-effective service. I have provided copyediting for many years, and I am now providing structural editing too.
